Digital Twins in Industry 4.0 and the Built Environment

A deep dive into security architectures, advanced threats, and defensive strategies for digital twins across smart manufacturing and smart buildings.

Abstract

Digital twins increasingly serve as the control-plane mirror of real-world industrial and building systems, synchronizing IoT and OT telemetry to drive optimization, predictive maintenance, and automation. This paper examines how that same synchronization expands the attack surface of cyber-physical systems — from data poisoning and IoT endpoint compromise to supply-chain and model-inversion risks.

It surveys secure digital-twin design principles across identity, network, data, and runtime layers, then maps them to concrete defensive strategies: continuous monitoring, anomaly detection, and incident response tailored to environments where a compromised twin can translate into physical-world disruption.

Key Topics Covered

  • Digital TwinsLifecycle, synchronization models, and benefits vs. operational/security tradeoffs.
  • Industry 4.0 IntegrationIoT/OT convergence, real-time telemetry, predictive analytics, and supply-chain dependencies.
  • Built Environment Use CasesSmart building automation, infrastructure monitoring, and energy/occupancy control.
  • Security ArchitecturesIdentity and access control, encryption, and network segmentation for twin components.
  • Advanced ThreatsTelemetry data poisoning, model inversion/leakage, endpoint compromise, and supply-chain attacks.
  • Defensive StrategiesAnomaly detection, behavioral baselines, incident response, and risk mitigation frameworks.
